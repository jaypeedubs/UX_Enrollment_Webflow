Hearing Preservation Techniques in Cochlear Implant Surgery

This lecture reviews evidence-based techniques for preserving residual low-frequency hearing during cochlear implant electrode insertion. We discuss soft surgical technique, slow insertion velocities, the round window approach, and the role of intraoperative ECochG.

Learning objectives

  • Distinguish the principal mechanisms of insertion trauma — direct trauma, hydraulic trauma, and inflammatory cascade.
  • Describe the soft surgical technique pioneered by Lehnhardt and refinements over the past two decades.
  • Compare round-window and cochleostomy approaches with respect to hearing preservation outcomes.
  • Interpret intraoperative electrocochleography (ECochG) findings during insertion.

Key references

  • Adunka OF, Buchman CA. Cochlear implant electrode arrays: hearing preservation strategies. Otol Neurotol 2018.
  • Roland JT et al. Soft surgical technique and the round window approach. Laryngoscope 2014.
  • Carlson ML et al. Intraoperative ECochG monitoring during CI surgery. Otol Neurotol 2017.
